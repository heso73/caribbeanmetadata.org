Founding Declaration

What we proclaim

We, peoples, creators, guardians, storytellers, artisans of images and sounds, united by winds, languages, memories and islands, declare that the Caribbean is not a fragment of the world: it is a world entire.

Today, we reclaim the word. Today, we reclaim the classification. Today, we reclaim sovereignty.

ARTICLE 1 — The reason for being

The CMS is established as the first global standard designed to index, protect, transmit and amplify Caribbean cultural sovereignty. It is not a tool. It is not a catalogue. It is a cultural institution, a common language, an act of sovereignty.

ARTICLE 4 — The Caribbean as matrix

The CMS recognises the Caribbean as an archipelago of languages, a continent of memories, an ocean of narratives, a civilisation in motion. The Caribbean defines itself. The CMS is its alphabet.

ARTICLE 6 — The right to exist in light

We affirm that every Caribbean work, whether great or modest, ancient or new, local or diasporic, has the right to a name, a place, a memory, visibility, recognition. The CMS is the place where these rights become reality.

CONCLUSION

The Caribbean no longer waits to be discovered. It declares itself. It structures itself. It tells its own story. It standardises itself. The CMS is its digital birth certificate and its act of cultural renaissance.
