Taxonomy

The 6 metadata families

Each family structures a distinct aspect of Caribbean identity. Together, they form a complete system of cultural classification.

01 · Linguistic 02 · Cultural 03 · Narrative 04 · Rhythmic 05 · Geographic 06 · Socio-Historical
Family 01

Linguistic metadata

Describes the languages, dialects, registers and language practices present in Caribbean audiovisual content.

TYPICAL USE
Index a film shot in Guadeloupean Creole with passages in French and English, incorporating traditional oral proverbs.

Creoles

  • Guadeloupean Creole
  • Martinican Creole
  • Haitian Creole
  • Réunion Creole

Dialects

  • Jamaican Patois
  • Trinidadian English
  • Papiamento
  • Sranan Tongo

Practices

  • Code-switching
  • Code alternation
  • Rhythmic orality

Oral forms

  • Creole proverbs
  • Oral tales
  • Verbal jousting
  • Caribbean griot
Family 02

Cultural metadata

Describes cultural practices, traditions, and spiritual, musical and culinary heritages specific to the Caribbean.

TYPICAL USE
Classify a documentary series on the Guadeloupe carnival incorporating syncretic religious rites and gwoka music.

Spiritualities

  • Haitian Vodou
  • Santería
  • Rastafari
  • Afro-Caribbean syncretism

Traditions

  • Carnival
  • Traditional Mas'
  • Léwoz / Gwoka
  • Bèlè

Music

  • Reggae
  • Zouk
  • Kompa
  • Calypso
  • Dancehall

Diaspora & Identity

  • Diasporic identity
  • African heritage
  • Cultural métissage
Family 03

Narrative metadata

Describes genres, dramatic structures and narrative archetypes specific to the Caribbean storytelling tradition.

TYPICAL USE
Index a feature film blending Caribbean magic realism, postcolonial political thriller and intergenerational family saga.

Genres

  • Caribbean magic realism
  • Tropical thriller
  • Social drama
  • Creole comedy

Structures

  • Family saga
  • Urban chronicles
  • Coming-of-age narrative
  • Circular narration

Archetypes

  • The storyteller figure
  • Street hero
  • Ancestor guide
  • Returning exile

Themes

  • Collective memory
  • Resistance
  • Emancipation
Family 04

Rhythmic metadata

Describes the tempo, cadence, musicality of dialogue and sound patterns that characterise Caribbean content.

TYPICAL USE
Qualify the rhythm of a music documentary where spoken interventions follow a percussive cadence rooted in the gwoka tradition.

Tempo

  • Slow insular tempo
  • Fast urban tempo
  • Contemplative tempo

Cadence

  • Syncopated dialogue
  • Percussive speech
  • Call & response rhythm

Musicality

  • Sung intonation
  • Musical orality
  • Meaningful silence

Sound patterns

  • Tropical ambiance
  • Urban soundscape
  • Insular nature sounds
Family 05

Geographic metadata

Locates content within Caribbean space — territory, environment, landscape — with cultural rather than merely cartographic precision.

TYPICAL USE
Locate a web series shot in the working-class neighbourhoods of Pointe-à-Pitre, in coastal urban Guadeloupe.

Territories

  • Guadeloupe
  • Martinique
  • Haiti
  • Jamaica
  • Trinidad & Tobago
  • Cuba

Environments

  • Caribbean urban
  • Tropical rural
  • Island coastal
  • Tropical forest

Cultural spaces

  • Greater Caribbean
  • Lesser Antilles
  • Caribbean diaspora

Symbolic landscapes

  • The sea as border
  • The plantation
  • The morne (hill)
Family 06

Socio-historical metadata

Contextualises content within the social, historical and political dynamics that have shaped the Caribbean and its peoples.

TYPICAL USE
Classify a film exploring intergenerational tensions between a Haitian diaspora family in New York and its roots.

History

  • Memory of slavery
  • Colonisation
  • Abolition
  • Independence

Identity

  • Creolisation
  • Postcoloniality
  • Négritude
  • Antillanité

Migration

  • Voluntary exile
  • Return home
  • Diaspora / island tensions

Social dynamics

  • Intergenerational tensions
  • Social class
  • Gender & power
