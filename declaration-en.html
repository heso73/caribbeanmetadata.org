Founding Declaration

CARIBBEAN METADATA STANDARD  ·  FOUNDING ACT v1.0

Adopted in the year when the Caribbean decides
to name itself.

Read the declaration
Preamble

We, peoples, creators, guardians, storytellers,
artisans of images and sounds,
united by winds, languages, memories and islands,
declare that the Caribbean is not a fragment of the world:
it is a world entire.

A world that has too often been described by others,
classified by others,
named by others.

Today, we reclaim the word. Today, we reclaim the classification. Today, we reclaim sovereignty.
ARTICLE 1
The reason
for being

The reason for being

The CMS is established as the first global standard designed to index, protect, transmit and amplify Caribbean cultural sovereignty.

It is not a tool.
It is not a website.
It is not a catalogue.

  • It is a cultural institution
  • a common language
  • a framework of recognition
  • an act of sovereignty
ARTICLE 2
Definition
of the Standard

Definition of the Standard

The CMS constitutes a normative reference defining the metadata necessary for:

  • the identification of Caribbean cultural works
  • the recognition of languages, creoles and variations
  • the valorisation of territories, diasporas and heritages
  • the documentation of practices, crafts and know-how
  • the traceability of narratives, influences and cultural lineages

It is recognised as an open, sovereign, evolving standard, administered by a Caribbean cultural authority.

ARTICLE 3
Legitimacy
and Governance

Legitimacy and Governance

The CMS is placed under the responsibility of an independent Caribbean body, tasked with:

  • maintaining the standard
  • guaranteeing its integrity
  • ensuring its adoption
  • representing the Caribbean in international bodies
  • protecting cultural sovereignty against any external appropriation

This body acts on behalf of the Caribbean cultural community, and not of any state, corporation or interest group.

ARTICLE 4
The Caribbean
as matrix

The Caribbean as matrix

The CMS recognises the Caribbean as:

  • an archipelago of languages
  • a continent of memories
  • an ocean of narratives
  • a civilisation in motion

It affirms that Caribbean culture cannot be reduced to imported categories, generic metadata, or foreign classifications.

The Caribbean defines itself.
The CMS is its alphabet.
ARTICLE 5
Mission
and scope

Mission and scope

The CMS has the mission:

  • to ensure the global discoverability of Caribbean works
  • to create a sovereign digital ecosystem
  • to enable platforms to integrate the Caribbean into their catalogues
  • to train future generations in cultural documentation
  • to build a lasting digital heritage

It is destined to become the international reference for any work originating from the Caribbean or its diaspora.

ARTICLE 6
The right
to exist in light

The right to exist in light

We affirm that every Caribbean work, whether great or modest, ancient or new, local or diasporic, has the right to:

  • a name
  • a place
  • a memory
  • visibility
  • recognition
The CMS is the place
where these rights become reality.
ARTICLE 7
Entry
into force

Entry into force

By this declaration, we officially proclaim the birth of the:

CMS — Global Standard of
Caribbean Cultural Sovereignty

And we invite:

  • creators
  • institutions
  • platforms
  • schools
  • festivals
  • peoples

to join this collective work.

Conclusion
The Caribbean no longer waits to be discovered. It declares itself. It structures itself. It tells its own story. It standardises itself. It emancipates itself.

The CMS is its digital birth certificate.
And its act of cultural renaissance.
