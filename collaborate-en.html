Partnerships

Let's build it together

The CMS is a collaborative standard. Its strength depends on the diversity and quality of those who carry it — creators, platforms, institutions, researchers.

Who it's for

Who can collaborate?

Each profile brings a specific contribution to the CMS ecosystem.

Platforms

Netflix · Amazon · Disney+

Integrate the CMS into your indexing systems to improve the discoverability of Caribbean content and access new cultural markets.

Institutions

Ministries · Local authorities · NGOs

Participate in the governance of the standard and contribute to the institutional anchoring of the CMS in Caribbean cultural policy.

Universities

Research & Academia

Bring the academic rigour necessary for the linguistic and cultural validation of CMS metadata families.

Studios

Studios · Producers · Creators

Tag your content according to the CMS standard to improve its visibility on global platforms and obtain certification.

Linguists

Linguists · Translators

Contribute to the multilingual extension of the standard — Spanish, Dutch, English — and to the precision of cultural definitions.

Researchers

Researchers · Data Scientists

Work on Caribbean corpora, participate in developing the CMS AI Engine and publish research on digital cultural sovereignty.

What we offer

What you gain by collaborating with the CMS

→

Early access to the standard

Founding partners have access to working versions of the CMS before official publication.

→

Official credit

Your organisation is cited in the White Paper and on the official CMS website.

→

Governance participation

Institutional partners participate in the standard's validation committee.

→

Implementation support

The Language Lab accompanies partners in integrating the CMS into their workflows.

→

Access to datasets

Research partners have access to Caribbean corpora built by the Language Lab.

→

CMS-Compliant label

Certified content displays the official Caribbean Metadata Standard seal.
